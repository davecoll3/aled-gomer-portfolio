About

A very warm welcome to you, as I’m sure you’ve guessed, my name is Aled.

Do read on, if you so please.


I’m primarily an actor, but I also write and make theatre and facilitate in the community.


I trained at the Royal Welsh College of Music and Drama and graduated with a first in acting - posh! Last year I trained in clowning at École Philippe Gaulier, which was kindly funded by the Carne Trust.


I have since been awarded an Arts Council of Wales Create Grant to develop my play Dad, Where’s My Rolex?. Had my theatrical debut at the Chichester Festival Theatre and voiced the character of a Pink Block in a kids Animations on S4C. Scroll down for the Spotlight if you wanna know more.


I was an Associate Artist for Pentabus Theatre; Pentabus is a wonderful theatre company that tours new writing with rural communities. The work they do is vital. Before this I participated on both the National and Local Pentabus Young Writers Group’s. They took me seriously as a writer, and in turn I took my writing seriously.


My most profound experiences in theatre have been through the lens of comedy, through clown and improvisation. I’ve began cultivating both into my growing practice, but there’s more to harvest. Where can clown be used in participatory work? How can improvisation help people with addiction? Can devised comedy be used with older people in our communities?


I grew up on a council estate in Cardiff and I consider myself working class. It’s my culture. I want to show that anyone from this background can engage with and work in the arts, if that’s what they fancy.


Theatre is my church, it’s where we share our stories.

Projects

The Mastiff

The Mastiff is a digital-theatre monologue and, you guessed it, written by me.


The Mastiff grapples with love (or lack of) and the loneliness of an older woman in the South Wales valleys.


Performed by Anita Reynolds
Written by Aled Gomer
Directed by Elle While

Learning to Float

Learning to Float is a short play written by moi.


It explores the struggles that gay people growing up in rural areas can tackle.

Facilitating

Below ye shall find my growing collaborations, relationships and connections with the community. I think theatre can truly free us up and let us be; it’s a brilliant tool.


I facilitated a set of creative writing workshops for the members of Recovery Cymru, alongside my DDad, Where’s My Rolex? research and development process. Recovery Cymru is an incredible charity that support people through their recovery journey. I urge you take a look at the brilliant work they do.


I have connection with PaperTrail. Firstly, I took part in their Creative Steps focus group to help them discover future opportunities as a company. Secondly, I facilitated an acting through improvisation workshop, as part of the outreach that ran alongside their production of A Visit, by ...


Recently, I have been collaborating with Pentabus Theatre to help them deliver their Vibrant Shropshire creative writing and performance project with primary school kids.


I love volunteering. It’s good for the community and just as good for the soul.


I spent many a long year volunteering with the Scouts Association, working with young people aged six to ten.


Before training at the Royal Welsh College, I volunteered with Hijnix Theatre Company, supporting adults with Special Educations Needs in their weekly drama sessions.


I’m currently in the process of becoming an Age Uk Telephone Friendship Service volunteer. What fun!
